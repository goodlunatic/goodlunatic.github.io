Lecture 04（Denial of service attack）

Low Orbit Ion Cannon (LOIC)

  • The Low Orbit Ion Cannon is a tool commonly used to launch DoS and DDoS attacks. It was originally developed by Praetox Technology as a network stresstesting application, but it has since become open-source and is now mostly used with malicious intent. It is known for being a very user-friendly and accessible tool, and it gained notoriety for its use by members of hacktivist group Anonymous as well as users of the 4Chan forums.This tool puts the ability to launch DDoS attacks in the hands of users with very little technical knowledge. It is widely available for download and has a simple point-and-click interface, additionally users can even launch attacks from a web browser using a JavaScript version called JS LOIC and a web version known as the Low Orbit Web Cannon.

Ping Death

  • Ping Death, also known as the Ping of Death, is a type of cyber attack that involves sending an unusually large or malformed Internet Control Message Protocol (ICMP) packet to a computer or network. ICMP is a network protocol used by network devices to send error messages and operational information about network conditions. In a Ping Death attack, the attacker sends an ICMP packet that exceeds the maximum allowed packet size (65,535 bytes). When the target system receives this oversized packet, it may not be able to handle it properly, leading to various issues such as system crashes, freezes, or network disruptions. In some cases, older or poorly configured systems might be vulnerable to Ping Death attacks because they are unable to handle exceptionally large packets. Modern operating systems and network devices have implemented security measures to prevent Ping Death attacks by filtering out oversized ICMP packets. However, it’s still crucial for organizations and individuals to keep their systems updated and properly configured to defend against potential vulnerabilities.

【What is a Firewall and why is it used? 】

A firewall is a network security device used to monitor and control the incoming and outgoing traffic in a network. It determines, based on predefined rule sets, whether to allow or block data packets.

The primary purpose of a firewall is to protect a network from unauthorized access and various types of network attacks by enforcing access control, isolating networks, and providing monitoring capabilities to enhance overall network security.

【What is the need of using firewall?】

Firewalls are essential for protecting networks and sensitive data from threats and unauthorized access. They filter incoming and outgoing traffic, control access to resources, prevent internal attacks, audit and record network traffic, block malware, and protect network devices. By using firewalls, organizations can enhance the security of their network and ensure only authorized users and data can access the network.

What is a three-way handshake?

The “three-way handshake” refers to the three-step process that takes place when establishing a TCP connection.

Initially, the client sends a connection request (SYN) to the server.
Upon receiving the request, the server responds with an acknowledgment (ACK) and sends its own connection request (SYN).
Finally, the client acknowledges (ACK) the server’s request, completing the establishment of the connection.

Which type of attacks is possible on a packet filtering firewall?

Possible attacks on a packet filtering firewall include:

  1. IP Spoofing: Attackers may attempt to deceive the firewall by disguising their IP address, trying to bypass the packet filter by impersonating a trusted host.
  2. Port Scanning: Attackers try to detect open network ports on the target system to identify potential vulnerabilities.
  3. Packet Fragmentation Attacks: Through segmentation and deceptively reassembling packets, attackers attempt to bypass the detection mechanisms of packet filters.
  4. Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks: Attackers may overload the network resources of the target system, rendering it unable to function properly.

在分组过滤防火墙上可能发生的攻击类型包括：

  1. IP地址欺骗（IP Spoofing）：攻击者伪装自己的IP地址，试图通过冒充信任的主机来绕过分组过滤器。
  2. 端口扫描（Port Scanning）：攻击者尝试探测目标系统上开放的网络端口，以发现潜在的漏洞。
  3. 数据包片段攻击（Packet Fragmentation Attacks）：通过分割和欺骗性地重新组装数据包，攻击者试图绕过分组过滤器的检测。
  4. 拒绝服务攻击（Denial-of-Service, DoS）：攻击者试图通过超载目标系统的网络资源，使其无法正常运作。

How does a traditional packet filtermake filtering decision?

The decision-making process of a traditional packet filter is based on a rule set and the basic attributes of the packet, such as source IP address, destination IP address, source port, destination port, and protocol type. Here is a brief overview of how a traditional packet filter makes filtering decisions:

  1. Check Source IP Address: The packet filter first examines the source IP address of the packet to determine its origin.
  2. Check Destination IP Address: Next, the packet filter checks the destination IP address of the packet to determine its destination.
  3. Check Protocol Type: The packet filter identifies the protocol type used by the packet, such as TCP, UDP, or ICMP.
  4. Check Source and Destination Ports: If the packet uses the TCP or UDP protocol, the packet filter examines the source and destination port numbers.
  5. Compare with Rule Set: The packet filter compares the attributes of the packet with a predefined rule set. If there is a match, it makes a filtering decision based on the policy specified in the rule (allow or deny).

Traditional packet filters often lack tracking of network connection states, making them susceptible to certain advanced attacks. To address more sophisticated threats, modern firewalls typically incorporate deep packet inspection and other advanced technologies to enhance network security.
传统分组过滤器的过滤决策主要基于规则集和数据包的基本属性，如源IP地址、目标IP地址、源端口、目标端口以及协议类型等。以下是传统分组过滤器如何做出过滤决策的简要步骤：

  1. 检查源IP地址：分组过滤器首先检查数据包的源IP地址，以确定它的来源。
  2. 检查目标IP地址：接下来，分组过滤器检查数据包的目标IP地址，以确定它的目的地。
  3. 检查协议类型：分组过滤器确定数据包所使用的协议类型，如TCP、UDP或ICMP。
  4. 检查源和目标端口：如果数据包使用的是TCP或UDP协议，分组过滤器将检查源和目标端口号。
  5. 比较规则集：分组过滤器将数据包的属性与预定义的规则集进行比较，如果有匹配的规则，则根据规则的策略（允许或拒绝）做出相应的过滤决策。
    传统分组过滤器通常缺乏对网络连接状态的跟踪，因此容易受到一些高级攻击的影响。为了应对更复杂的威胁，现代防火墙通常结合深度包检测和其他高级技术来提高网络安全性。

Stack Smashing and Stack Frame:

  1. Stack Smashing: Stack smashing, or buffer overflow, is a common security vulnerability that occurs when a program attempts to write too much data into the stack space. Attackers may exploit this by crafting inputs that cause a buffer overflow, overwriting critical information such as function return addresses, potentially leading to the execution of malicious code. Stack smashing is a common attack technique, as seen in buffer overflow attacks.

  2. Stack Frame: A stack frame is a memory block allocated on the stack during the execution of a function, used to store local variables, function parameters, and other information related to function execution. A stack frame typically includes a local variable area, a return address, and other control information. The creation and destruction of stack frames during function calls and returns are crucial parts of stack management.

Stack Smashing和Stack Frame：

  1. Stack Smashing（栈溢出）： 栈溢出是一种常见的安全漏洞，发生在程序试图将过多数据写入栈空间时。攻击者可能通过精心构造的输入导致栈溢出，覆盖了函数返回地址等关键信息，从而可能执行恶意代码。栈溢出是一种常见的攻击技术，如缓冲区溢出攻击。

  2. Stack Frame（栈帧）： 栈帧是程序在执行函数时在栈上分配的内存块，用于存储局部变量、函数参数和其他与函数执行相关的信息。栈帧通常包含局部变量区域、返回地址和其他控制信息。在函数调用和返回过程中，栈帧的创建和销毁是栈管理的关键部分。

13、Define defensive programming. When does a buffer overflow occur?

防御性编程（Defensive Programming）：

防御性编程是一种软件开发方法，旨在提高程序的稳定性和安全性，以防范潜在的错误和异常。它包括采用良好的编程实践、输入验证、错误处理、边界检查等策略，以减少软件系统中的缺陷和漏洞，增强程序对异常情况的容忍性。

缓冲区溢出（Buffer Overflow）：

缓冲区溢出是一种安全漏洞，发生在程序试图将超出预分配缓冲区容量的数据写入缓冲区时。这通常导致超出缓冲区末尾的数据覆盖了相邻内存区域，可能影响程序的正常执行。攻击者可通过巧妙构造的输入触发缓冲区溢出，覆盖关键数据（如函数返回地址），从而执行恶意代码。缓冲区溢出是一种常见的安全漏洞，特别是在处理用户输入时，对边界检查和输入验证的不足可能导致这种类型的漏洞。

Defensive Programming:

Defensive programming is a software development approach aimed at enhancing the stability and security of a program by anticipating and guarding against potential errors and exceptions. It involves adopting good programming practices, input validation, error handling, boundary checks, and other strategies to reduce defects and vulnerabilities in a software system, thereby improving the program’s resilience to exceptional conditions.

Buffer Overflow:

Buffer overflow is a security vulnerability that occurs when a program attempts to write data beyond the allocated capacity of a buffer. This typically leads to the overwriting of adjacent memory regions beyond the buffer’s end, potentially disrupting the normal execution of the program. Attackers can exploit buffer overflow by crafting inputs that trigger the overflow, overwriting critical data such as function return addresses, and enabling the execution of malicious code. Buffer overflow is a common security vulnerability, particularly when inadequate boundary checks and input validation are present, especially in the handling of user inputs.

14、Define the Internet of things. What is the IoT security framework?

物联网（Internet of Things，IoT）：

物联网是指通过互联网连接、互相通信的物理设备和物体的网络。这些物联网设备可以包括传感器、智能设备、嵌入式系统等，通过网络进行数据交换，实现信息共享和自动化操作。物联网的目标是实现各种设备的智能化，提高生活和工作的效率。

物联网安全框架：

物联网安全框架是一套旨在保护物联网生态系统免受潜在威胁的安全措施和标准。该框架通常包括以下方面的考虑：

  1. 身份验证和授权： 保证只有授权的设备和用户能够访问物联网系统。

  2. 数据隐私和加密： 采用加密技术确保在物联网传输的数据隐私得到保护。

  3. 设备管理和固件安全： 确保物联网设备的安全管理，包括固件的更新和漏洞修复。

  4. 网络安全： 保护物联网网络免受网络攻击，如拒绝服务攻击和入侵。

  5. 安全监控和响应： 建立监控系统，及时检测并应对潜在的安全威胁。

  6. 标准化和合规性： 制定符合行业标准和法规的安全标准，确保物联网系统的合规性。

这些安全措施旨在保障物联网生态系统中的设备、数据和网络的安全，防范潜在的风险和攻击。

Internet of Things (IoT):

The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to a network of physical devices and objects that are interconnected and communicate with each other through the internet. These IoT devices can include sensors, smart devices, embedded systems, etc., exchanging data over the network to enable information sharing and automation. The goal of IoT is to make various devices intelligent, enhancing efficiency in both personal and professional domains.

IoT Security Framework:

The IoT security framework is a set of security measures and standards designed to protect the IoT ecosystem from potential threats. This framework typically encompasses considerations such as:

  1. Authentication and Authorization: Ensuring that only authorized devices and users can access the IoT system.

  2. Data Privacy and Encryption: Employing encryption techniques to ensure the privacy of data transmitted within the IoT.

  3. Device Management and Firmware Security: Ensuring secure management of IoT devices, including firmware updates and vulnerability patches.

  4. Network Security: Protecting the IoT network from cyber threats such as denial-of-service attacks and intrusion.

  5. Security Monitoring and Response: Establishing monitoring systems to promptly detect and respond to potential security threats.

  6. Standardization and Compliance: Developing security standards that align with industry norms and regulations to ensure compliance within the IoT system.

These security measures aim to safeguard the devices, data, and networks within the IoT ecosystem, mitigating potential risks and attacks.

15 、List and briefly define the three broad classes of controls and the three categories each can include.

控制的三个广泛类别及其各自包含的三个类别：

  1. 管理性控制：

    • 政策和程序（Policies and Procedures）： 确定组织内部规范和操作程序，以支持信息安全目标。
    • 人员培训和认证（Personnel Training and Certification）： 提供员工所需的培训和认证，以确保他们理解和遵守信息安全政策。
    • 风险管理（Risk Management）： 识别、评估和应对信息安全风险，以减轻潜在的威胁。

  2. 技术性控制：

    • 访问控制（Access Control）： 确保只有经过授权的用户能够访问系统和数据。
    • 加密（Encryption）： 使用加密技术来保护敏感数据的机密性，防止未经授权的访问。
    • 网络和端点安全（Network and Endpoint Security）： 采取措施保护网络和设备免受恶意攻击，包括防火墙、反病毒软件等。

  3. 物理性控制：

    • 设备安全（Equipment Security）： 通过物理手段，如锁定和访问控制，保护设备免受未经授权的访问。
    • 环境控制（Environmental Controls）： 确保信息技术设备在适宜的环境条件下运行，防止物理损害。
    • 备份和恢复（Backup and Recovery）： 实施定期备份和灾难恢复计划，以防止数据丢失和业务中断。

Three Broad Classes of Controls and Their Three Categories Each:

  1. Management Controls:

    • Policies and Procedures: Establishing internal norms and operational procedures within the organization to support information security objectives.
    • Personnel Training and Certification: Providing employees with necessary training and certifications to ensure understanding and compliance with information security policies.
    • Risk Management: Identifying, assessing, and addressing information security risks to mitigate potential threats.

  2. Technical Controls:

    • Access Control: Ensuring that only authorized users can access systems and data.
    • Encryption: Using encryption techniques to protect the confidentiality of sensitive data and prevent unauthorized access.
    • Network and Endpoint Security: Implementing measures to safeguard networks and devices from malicious attacks, including firewalls, antivirus software, and more.

  3. Physical Controls:

    • Equipment Security: Protecting devices from unauthorized access through physical means such as locking mechanisms and access controls.
    • Environmental Controls: Ensuring that information technology equipment operates under suitable environmental conditions to prevent physical damage.
    • Backup and Recovery: Implementing regular backup and disaster recovery plans to prevent data loss and business interruptions.
